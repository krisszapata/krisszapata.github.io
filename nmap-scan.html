Local Network Port Scan with Nmap

Overview

I targeted my router to identify any open ports, services, and potential vulnerabilities. The goal was to understand basic network enumeration.

What I Did

  • Used Nmap’s -sV scan to detect open ports and service versions on the router.
  • Interpreted scan results, including identification of services like DNS, HTTP, and UPnP.
  • Verified filtered ports (such as Telnet and SSH) to assess the security posture of the network.
  • Confirmed router responded to ping (ICMP), suggesting it’s discoverable on the network.

Lessons Learned

  • Learned how to use Nmap for basic scanning and service fingerprinting.
  • Gained experience interpreting scan outputs and identifying potential risks.
  • Recognized the importance of router hardening, such as disabling UPnP and ensuring unused ports are closed.
